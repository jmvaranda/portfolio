Mobile music answer

This feature involved leveraging Bing’s music answer on desktop to the mobile experience .

  • View collection

Design challenge

The main challenge for this particular feature was exploring solutions on how content would be ordered when a user would search for music whether by artist, album, or song title.

  • View collection

Wireframing & exploration

During the exploration process, I found that a swipeable list affordance would be the most engaging and discoverable interaction to list music content.

Depending upon selecting a song or album, a requery would occur showing the user either a song answer with a video – or a list view of songs. The single song answer also had the song list above it as well with the current song highlighted.

Visual iteration

Visually, this experience was built using mostly common controls for the text styles. The swipeable list interaction was a new custom design affordance I created allowing users to discover content quickly.

One of the product requirements was that the mobile music answer be available on Windows Phone. The Windows Phone music answer design was built following the WP common controls and visual guidelines.

To view feature live: on a mobile device, open your browser and search for "artist name" and "album" on bing.com

Tonglen Lake Website

Tonglen Lake is an exclusive luxury lodge located in Denali, Alaska.

Design challenge

Client wanted a unique experience that showcased the unique features of the Tonglen Lake lodge offered.

Visual exploration & implementation

To organize the amount of content the client wanted on the website, an information architecture was created to establish hierarchy. A one page experience was chosen to illustrate the story featuring a snapshot of each element of the lodge.

Working with Tonglen lake,, we had access to great photography and imagery to leverage visually. Strong use of photography was used to showcase the landscapes surrounding the lodge. Textures of wood and artisan crafts were also featured in the design explorations.

A parallax scrolling effect was taken advantage of when the user scrolls or navigates through the one-page experience. The final design went through several revisions, I also assisted with developing the front-end markup.
